# FoodOrderV1 v1.7.0

v1.7.0 is the high-impact product release that evolves FoodOrderV1 from a technically mature collaborative bucket application into an organizer-first group-order platform with explicit live sessions, lower-friction invitation and guest activation, settlement and reconciliation workflows, monetization foundations, measurable product funnels, stronger privacy boundaries, and a complete responsive experience review.

## Release objectives

- Keep architecture and maintainability at a release-evidenced 10/10.
- Keep automated testing and quality gates at a release-evidenced 10/10.
- Raise security/privacy, acquisition/activation, analytics/observability, and monetization readiness above 8/10.
- Raise organizer productivity and responsive UI/UX above 9/10.
- Preserve existing v1.6.x Firebase and local-device data through additive schemas, normalizers, migrations, characterization tests, and rollback guidance.

## Required product scope

- Reusable menu templates separated from independent live order sessions.
- Explicit participant pending, viewed, ordering, done, skipped, and removed states.
- Public minimal-data invitation preview and secure scoped guest participation.
- Account linking that preserves guest contributions.
- Organizer command center with readiness, reminders, deadline, lock, handoff, payment, and settlement visibility.
- Recurring sessions, repeat selections, idempotent scheduling, and timezone-safe reminders.
- Restaurant-ready WhatsApp, print/PDF, and CSV handoff outputs without unnecessary participant PII.
- Integer minor-unit financial authority, manual payment tracking, private proof attachments, and deterministic actual-receipt reconciliation.
- Free, Organizer Pro, and Business Workspace plan definitions with server-authoritative entitlements and provider-neutral billing contracts.
- Consent-aware typed analytics, error/performance observability, correlation IDs, and feature kill switches.
- Privacy controls covering analytics preferences, account export/deletion, retention, and shared-record anonymization policy.
- Full mobile, landscape, tablet, desktop, ultrawide, dark-mode, English, Arabic, RTL, keyboard, and accessibility validation.

## Engineering and governance

- The source version is `1.7.0` on the version branch and pull request.
- Green branch builds receive immutable prerelease artifact versions such as `1.7.0-dev.<run>`; CI never creates self-triggering version commits.
- Every version branch checkout is synchronized through the branch-version skill and guarded again at commit, push, and CI.
- Every application dependency remains behind its owned package facade.
- No architecture-rule suppression, skipped tests, client-authoritative security, or undocumented exception is permitted.
- Existing canonical rules, skills, context, memory, migration records, module documentation, and generated knowledge must remain synchronized with implemented behavior.

## Known external-environment limitations

- Native iOS compilation, signing, entitlement validation, and App Store validation require macOS/Xcode and must not be claimed without an executed native run.
- Production subscription checkout, email delivery, push delivery, and optional OCR require their real provider credentials. The release must still include secure contracts, validation, deterministic test adapters, disabled/unconfigured UX, and operations documentation.
- Privacy-policy and terms wording requires legal review; product and data behavior must nevertheless be documented precisely.
